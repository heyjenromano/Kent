🚆 Trains
Southeastern Railway — Bike Policy

Free, no reservation needed — first come, first served. Bikes near the accessible toilet / flip-down seats. Wheelchair users always have priority.

💡 Key restriction: non-folding bikes cannot board services leaving London between 4–7 pm on weekdays. Travelling after 7 pm on Friday is fine. Sundays have no restrictions.

🍽 Dinner Recommendations

All worth booking ahead in July.

Friday Night — Whitstable
⭐ Top pick

The Old Neptune

Iconic weather-boarded pub on the shingle beach. Fresh oysters, locally caught fish, Shepherd Neame ales. CAMRA Good Beer Guide. 5-min stroll from Boat House — stunning at sunset.

📍 Marine Terrace, CT5 1EJ — book for a beach-facing table

Alternative

Samphire

Beloved all-day bistro celebrating Kentish produce. Warm and characterful. Recently under new stewardship but same team and spirit. Good for something more considered after the journey.

📍 4 High Street, CT5 1BQ — book online

Saturday Night — Broadstairs
⭐ Michelin recommended

The Tartar Frigate

18th-century pub above Viking Bay. Beer-battered cod, local shellfish, stunning sea views. Michelin Guide Recommended 2025 & CAMRA Good Beer Guide 2024.

📍 Harbour Street, CT10 1EU — book a window table

Alternative

Albariño Tapas

Frequently cited as Broadstairs' best restaurant. Excellent Spanish small plates and natural wine list. Very popular with locals — essential to book ahead in summer.

📍 Broadstairs town centre — book well ahead

Also in Broadstairs

Kebbells (8 Victoria Parade) — Med-leaning spot overlooking the harbour, great for fresh fish daily specials. Good backup if the others are full.

Sunday Lunch — Deal 🎉
⭐ Top pick

81 Beach Street

Right on Deal's seafront. Seasonal British menu with excellent fresh fish and local produce. Open from noon daily. Sunday lunches particularly well reviewed.

📍 Beach Street, CT14 · 01304 368136 · Book ahead

Alternative

The Port Arms

Beautiful seafront pub with a terrace. Great for a relaxed post-ride pint and bite. Terrace is first come, first served — no booking needed.

📍 10 Beach Street, CT14 7AH

🏁 Start: Whitstable

Depart ~09:30

Head east from Whitstable Harbour along the seafront. The famous beach huts and Tankerton Slopes make a lovely warm-up. Follow NCN15 / Oyster Bay Trail signs east toward Herne Bay.

Herne Bay

~8 km

Beautiful Victorian seafront and the third-longest pier in England. Grab a coffee at the bandstand café on the prom before pressing on.

Cumulative: 8 km

🏛 Reculver Towers & Roman Fort

Don't miss

Dramatic twin medieval towers on the clifftop above a 3rd-century Roman fort. HatsHats café right beside the towers is a famous cyclists' stop for coffee and snacks.

Cumulative: ~13 km

Birchington & Westgate-on-Sea

Quiet coastal lanes

Continues along the northern seawall and quiet coastal roads. Open sea views almost continuously. Follow NCN15 / Viking Coastal Trail signage.

Cumulative: ~22 km

🍽 Lunch: Margate Old Town

~13:00

Peter's Fish Factory — legendary fish and chips near the harbour. Or the Harbour Arms for a sit-down with sea views. Margate's Old Town has a brilliant food scene. Optional: Turner Contemporary gallery (free entry) is worth a brief look.

Cumulative: ~26 km

🏖 Botany Bay & Kingsgate Bay

Swim stop — don't skip!

The chalk cliff coves in your photo — white stack formations, golden sand, turquoise shallows. Lock bikes and walk down for a swim. One of the true highlights of the trip.

Cumulative: ~31 km

🏁 Arrive: Broadstairs

~16:00 · 35 km

Gentle descent into Broadstairs and Viking Bay — the beach in your other photo! Check in at Pierremont Rooms, 100 Pierremont Avenue. Freshen up, then enjoy the town before dinner.

🏁 Start: Broadstairs

Depart ~10:00

Head south toward Ramsgate on NCN Route 1. A short 5 km coastal hop with views across the bay.

⚓ Ramsgate Royal Harbour

Worth a pause

One of England's few Royal Harbours, granted by George IV. Beautiful and often overlooked — elegant Victorian buildings above a working harbour. Allow 10 minutes. Pick up NCN1 inland from here.

Cumulative: ~5 km

Pegwell Bay & Orchard Lanes

Countryside transition

Coast gives way to classic Kent countryside — flat, quiet lanes through hamlets and orchards. Pass Pegwell Bay, an important Viking landing site.

Cumulative: ~15 km

🏰 Sandwich — Medieval Town

Lock bikes & explore

One of England's best-preserved medieval towns. Crooked timber-framed streets, a working Barbican gate. Allow 20–30 minutes. Richborough Roman Fort is 2 km north if you have the energy.

Cumulative: ~20 km

☕ Coffee stop: Sandwich

Optional refuel

Several good independent cafés in Sandwich. Worth a stop before the final flat ~15 km south to Deal through orchard country.

Cumulative: ~20 km

🏁 Arrive: Deal

~13:00 · 30 km

You've done it! Great independent shops, a long pebble beach, and Deal Castle. Head to 81 Beach Street for a celebratory lunch on the seafront, then train home at ~15:00.

📱 Route planning apps

Download GPX files before leaving London. Both routes are well documented on Komoot (search "Whitstable to Broadstairs coastal" and "Broadstairs to Deal via Sandwich"). Ride with GPS and OS Maps have good NCN15 and NCN1 coverage.

⚠ Parts of the Herne Bay and Margate promenades may require dismounting past beach huts in summer. Brief, but worth knowing.
